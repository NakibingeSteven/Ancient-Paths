1948 Restoration of Isreal State(Which Isreal?)

Restoration in 1948

On May 14, empowered by a resolution of the United Nations, the Zionist Movement proclaimed the rebirth of the State of Israel. For almost 2,000 years the Jewish people had been “wanderers among the nations.” Now they were home. Yet their struggles had just begun.

An Arab League composed of Egyptians, Iraqis, Syrians, and Jordanians quickly invaded Palestine in an attempt to crush out the new nation

Yet by 1949 the Arabs were defeated, and Israel was still in the land. In May of 1967, Egypt, Jordan, and Syria prepared for another attack. The Israelis struck first, and the war was over in six days. In 1973, at the beginning of the Jewish season of Yom Kippur, the Egyptians and Syrians attacked again. The battles were fierce and bloody. Yet by 1974, Israel was again on top and still in the land.

For more than 50 years these astonishing events have gripped the attention of much of the Christian world. A conclusion has been reached by millions: This must be the fulfillment of Bible prophecy. The rebirth of the State of Israel in 1948 is now considered by countless Christians to be the most significant prophetic event of the 20th century.

In many ways, the 1948 theory is like the Titanic. In the minds of countless believers, it is considered unsinkable. A true witness to the truth of the importance of literal state of Isreal in end time events .However, in a few moments, when this popular theoryhits the ice of God's Word, it sinks since it has no real biblical basis

Arguments for 1948

Three main arguments are used to support the theory that Bible prophecy was fulfilled in 1948.


Budding of the fig tree

The passage quoted for this is

"Let the fig tree teach you a lesson. When its branches become green and tender and it starts putting out leaves, you know that summer is near. In the same way, when you see all these things, you will know that the time is near, ready to begin.(Mat 24:32-33 GNB, emphasis added)

The claim is that when the Jewish people…became a nation again on 14 May 1948 the 'fig tree' put forth its first leaves. This is far from the truth because a parallel passage in Luke says something different

Then Jesus told them this parable: "Think of the fig tree and all the other trees. When you see their leaves beginning to appear, you know that summer is near. In the same way, when you see these things happening, you will know that the Kingdom of God is about to come. (Luk 21:29-31 GNB)

Luke wrote, “The fig tree and all the trees” hence we clearly see Jesus did not have in mind just one tree representing Israel in 1948. The fig tree budding is not the sign but was used as an analogy for the signs of the end. It simply represents “all” the signs in Matthew 24, none of which is the specified rebirth of Israel in 1948.


The miraculous victories of Isreal and end time regatheringargument

Many view the Israeli victories in 1949, 1967, and 1973 as strong evidence that God had regathered Israel and was now fighting for His chosen nation, even though the leadership of that nation still firmly rejects its own Messiah

The idea is now being expressed all around the globe that ancient prophecies which predicted regathering of Israel back to their land were fulfilled in 1948. The main prophecy quoted is found in Ezekiel chapters 36-38.

Again, God does not change (see Malachi 3:6). All throughout sacred history, He could not fight for Israel while they were in unbelief. Thus He could not have been fighting for the Jewish nation in 1949, 1967, and 1973.

Because a nation wins battles is not evidence in itself that God is fighting for that nation. Was God fighting for Hitler when he won so many battles? Was the Lord with the Nazis when they successfully incinerated so many Jews in crematoriums? Obviously not!


The reason for scattering

The reason Israel was scattered in Old Testament times was because she forsook God, broke His law, and disobeyed His word (see Jeremiah 16:10-13; 29:18-19). If you look carefully, according to the same Old Testament passages, Israel must first repent of her sins before such a regathering can be accomplished by God

God told Israel: “It shall come to pass, when all these things come upon you, the blessing and the curse…and you shall call them to mind among all the nations where the Lord your God drives you, and you shall return to the Lord your God and obey His voice…then the Lord your God will bring you back from captivity…and gather you again from all the nations” (Deuteronomy 30:1-3, KJV emphasis added).

'If you people of Israel are unfaithful to me, I will scatter you among the other nations. But then if you turn back to me and do what I have commanded you, I will bring you back to the place where I have chosen to be worshiped, even though you are scattered to the ends of the earth.'

(Neh 1:8-9 GNB,emphasis added)

“And you will seek Me and find Me, when you search for Me with all your heart. I will be found by you, says the Lord, and I will bring you back from your captivity; I will gather you from all the nations” (Jeremiah 29:13-14).

Ezekiel 36 the verse quoted as fulfilled in 1948 contains conditional elements that were not fulfilled.

“Thus says the Lord God: 'On the day that I cleanse you from all your iniquities, I will also enable you to dwell in the cities, and the ruins shall be rebuilt” (Ezekiel 36:33).

Thus, “on the day” God cleanses Israel from “all” of her sins, on that day He would “also enable” her to dwell in her cities. This did not happen in 1948.

Israel as a nation was not cleansed from “all” her iniquities at that time. Especially had it not forsaken its Heaven-confronting sin of rejecting God's Son (see Matthew 21:37-39).

Note:

Because the Messiah has come, this “return to the Lord” must include fully embracing Jesus Christ. The facts of history reveal that Jewish Zionism did not meet this spiritual condition in 1948.

These Scriptures are plain. Israel must first repent, then God will gather her. Once again, this condition was not met by the Zionist movement in 1948
