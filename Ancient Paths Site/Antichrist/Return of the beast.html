Return of the Beast

Prophecy predicts that the 'man of sin' would have two periods of dominion—one in the past and one in the future. The papacy would be freed from captivity to the civil power in 538 AD but would be thrown back into captivity by the same power in 1798 AD.

Paul did not explain that the 'man of sin' would be bound (before 538 AD) and then released (in 538 AD) and bound again (in 1798 AD) only to be released once more (by the beast from the earth in the near future).this part of the puzzle was added to the prophecy in Revelation 13

The prophecy of Revelation 13:9, 10, 3 indicates that the papacy will once again be freed from its captivity

The Bible warns “…his deadly wound was healed: and all the world wondered after the beast” (Revelation 13:3 KJV).

This literal view of a murdered and resurrected Mr. Sin has been depicted in scores of prophecy books. Although details vary, most modern portrayals involve the antichrist being shot with a gun and then miraculously being raised to life

The wound and captivity of the beast

The wounding of the beast is referred to four times in Revelation (13:3, 10, 12, and 14). Yet, the beast “was wounded by the sword and lived” (13:14, emphasis added). Thus his wound comes from “the sword,” not a pistol, rifle, or submachine gun.

Revelation 13:10 also reveals the additional insight that the wounding of the beast involves his going “into captivity.”

Healing the wound

The prophecies about “the little horn” and “the beast” do not stop with the 1,260- year period, the deadly “wound by the sword,” or the beast's “captivity.”

Scripture predicts, “…his deadly wound was healed: and all the world wondered after the beast” (Revelation 13:3 KJV)
