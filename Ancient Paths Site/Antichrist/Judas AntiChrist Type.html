Judas Antitype

This exact phrase, “the son of perdition,” occurs only twice in the entire New Testament. One of these is in 2 Thessalonians 2:3 about the antichrist:

“Let no one deceive you by any means; for that Day [the day when Jesus Christ returns to gather us] will not come unless the falling away comes first, and the man of sin is revealed, the son of perdition” (2 Thessalonians 2:3).

The only other time it is used is in John 17:12 when Jesus Christ applied it to one of His own disciples, Judas Iscariot, His betrayer:

While I was with them in the world, I kept them in thy name: those that thou gavest me I have kept, and none of them is lost, but the son of perdition; that the scripture might be fulfilled. (Joh 17:12 KJV)

Jesus referred to Judas Iscariot with the identical name of the ‘man of sin,’ so the character of the ‘man of sin’ must bear a marked resemblance to the character of Judas.

A comparison of Judas and the antichrist

Judas The antichrist
One of the twelve disciples. Part of Christ’s inner circle (see Luke 6:13-16). Part of the church (1 John 2:19)
Judas even received the power of God (see Matthew 10:1-4). Received Holy Spirit when part of the Church
Iscariot finally fell away from His Savior (Acts 1:25). Went out of the church (1 John 2:19)
Outward love for God, kissed the Lord after betraying him. They deny the Father and the Son (1 John 2:22)
Sold Jesus for money. Iscariot finally fell away from His Savior for 30 pieces of silver (see Acts 1:25). Give up truth for worldly pleasure and wealth
He was a zealot Misunderstood the work of Jesus, expecting a political messiah. Thought he was acting for Jesus yet he was against him. Unites with the political and religious leaders and believes in spreading the gospel through a church-state union
United with the Jewish religious leaders to capture Jesus (Matthew 26:49 KJV). Unites with the political and religious leaders to kill saints of God
Controlled by the devil (John 6:70, Joh 8:40-44). Do works of the devil (Joh 8:44)
