Joseph and Jesus Typology

Aspect Joseph Jesus
Miraculous birth Jacob was 90 years old, Rachel was barren Gen 30:22-24; 37:3 Mary was a virgin Mt 1:18; Lk 1:31-33
Both were shepherds of their father’s sheep Gen 37:2 The good shepherd John 10:11
Inspired by the Holy Spirit Gen 40:8; 41:38 The Spirit of the Lord is upon me, (Lk 4:18)
Sentenced with 2 criminals, one saved, one lost Cup bearer and baker (Gen 40:1-3) Two thieves (Lk 23:32-33)
Beloved son of Father Israel loved Joseph more than all his children (Gen. 37:3a) Beloved, only son of God (Matt.3:17b)
Envied and hated by their own without a cause And when his brethren saw that their father loved him more they…hated him… (Gen 37:4, 8) …the religious people and Jews (Mark 15:10). They hated me without a cause (John 15:25b). (Jn 7:5; 15:25; Mk 3:21)
Foretold their rule over brethren His parents and brothers (Gen.37:5-7) His enthronement in heaven (Matt.26:64b)
Called dreamers or mad and mocked The dreamer (Gen. 37:19). Mocked Gen 37:20 He is out of his mind (Mark 3:21b). Mocked Mt 27:43
Sent to check on his brothers By Abraham (Gen. 37:14a) By God (Luke 20:13b)
Goes to check on his brothers willingly And Joseph went after his brethren, and found them (Gen. 37:17b) … He came unto his own… (John 1:11a)
Rejected and condemned to die By his brothers (Gen. 37:18b) By his people. But they cried, saying, crucify him! Crucify him! (Luke 23:21)
Distinguished Special robe Robe of many colors with long sleeves Gen 37:3 Robe of righteousness makes word hate him Rev 3:5, Rev 19:8 And to her was granted that she should be arrayed in fine linen, clean and white: for the fine linen is the righteousness of saints.
Stripped of special Clothing Literal Special clothing stripped by brothers (Gen 37:23b). Spiritually called a sinner for his brother, the sacrificial lamb (Matt. 27:28a).
Literal robe stripped from him by disregard his robe of righteousness and call him a sinner (Matt. 27:28a).
Sold for silver Twenty pieces of silver; (Gen.37:28b). Thirty pieces of silver (Matt. 26:15b).
Became a servant before a ruler And Joseph was brought down to Egypt…and he was in the house of his master, the Egyptian (Gen. 39:1-2). I am among you as he that seventh (luke 22:27b). …took upon him the form of a servant (Phil. 2:7b).
Suffering was God's plan to save brothers Sent to Egypt to save his family Gen 45:5–7 Sent to save humanity (Acts 2:23; 3:18; Eph 3:11)
Resisted temptation Resists temptation from Potiphar's wife (Gen. 39:7-12). Resisted all temptation (Heb 4:15b).
Numbered with transgressors Imprisoned with king's prisoners (Gen. 39:20a). Crucified with sinners (Luke 23:33).
Promoted to second highest position Ruler over Egypt (Gen. 41:41) Gen 41:42-44 Sits on right hand of God (Mt 28:18, Phil. 2:9).
Began “ministry” at 30 years old Was thirty years old (Gen 41:46). Thirty years old (Lk 3:23)
Feed the world in a time of famine Sold food to the whole world (gen.41:57a). Spiritually he is the bread of life (john 6:35a).
Physically feed large crowds (Jn 6, Mat 14:21)
Changed from not recognized The ruler Joseph not recognized by his brothers (Gen. 42:8). The walk to Emmaus Luk 24:15,16
His brothers/persecuted feared what he become His brothers feared the most powerful man in Egypt (Gen. 45:5a). All knees will bow to Him, even those who killed him (zech. 12:10b). Phil 2:10
Revelation reconciliation Forgives his brothers (Gen.45:3b-9). Forgives Israel and all sinners who killed him and will reveal himself Luk 23:34
