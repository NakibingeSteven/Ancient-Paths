Typology Introduction

It is usually an Old Testament type prefiguring something about redemption in the New Testament. The sacrificial system, the feasts and the whole sanctuary system were designed to foretell the sacrificial, redemptive work of Christ. In this sense, a type, by definition, points to the future of Christ, the church or the world to come.

Forgotten Bible Study Method

Typology seems to be largely neglected in the modern church mainly due to The laws and restrictions of the Old Testament. People are so involved with the object of the Gospel that they have forgotten the shadow for the object. They reject the laws as if they were not given by the same God they worship in the New Testament. They cry "grace" as if the Lord acquired it later and it was never His trait in the Old Testament. The consequence is they miss out on the unfulfilled truths in the Old Testament stories, laws, and covenants.

The Characteristics of Typology

  • Correspondence / Similar Flow:

Just as a shadow follows the outlines and shape of the object, the type and antitype must have a similar flow of events or similarities in the area they are both depicting or demonstrating. Example: the type Passover lamb and antitype Christ were both blameless/innocent and substitutes for sin that is not theirs. Also, evil cannot be the type of what is good and pure.

  • Dual Historicity:

The types and antitypes both take place. In most cases, the type has a literal meaning at its point in time that is important. The antitype is just a secondary meaning that God has hidden in time and space about the event referring to the letter coming of His son.

Note: Allegory is dangerous and it is where some interpreters claim that the types (Old Testament stories) didnâ€™t happen and they are just symbolic stories (with no historical happening) written to predict the coming of Messiah. Hence they are just vessels with hidden spiritual meanings. In summary, it promotes single historicity antitype/fulfillment.

The allegory minimizes and often destroys the literal and historical in favor of the secondary sense.

  • Type is More Glorious than Object

The antitype is more perfect, greater, and continues. It reveals more truth when fulfilled and is more superior to the type. Christ can save; the blood of lambs and goats can't. Christ is superior to Melchizedek. Christ's redemptive work is greater than that of the Passover.

  • Predictiveness/Predictive Prophecy

Because of the correspondence or similar flow of events, if one knows the flow of the antitype, they can predict the flow of the antitype. Simply said, if the Jews had understood the Jewish sanctuary and sacrificial system, they would have understood the purpose and mission of the Messiah.

In a sense, the prophets knew about the antitype or future fulfillment.

THE DANGERS OF TYPOLOGY

How Much of the Old Testament Is Typical?

The extravagance of some of the early Fathers (Idealism)/false typology

The early Fathers, as Origen, Ambrose, and Jerome (revived in our time by Andrew Jukes and his imitators). They sought for types, and of course found them, in every incident and event, however trivial, recorded in Scripture.

Mystery and mysticism were seen everywhere, in the cords and pins of the tabernacle, in the yield of herds, in the death of one, in the marriage of another, even in the number of fish caught by the disciples on the night the risen Saviour appeared to them--how much some have tried to make of that number, 153!

The rejection of typology as a method of interpretation

This opinion assumes that the New Testament writers have exhausted the types of the Old Testament, while the fact is that those found in the later Scripture are but samples taken from the storehouse where many more are found. If they are not, then nothing is more arbitrary than the New Testament use of types, for there is nothing to distinguish them from a multitude of others of the same class.

Further, the view assumes that divine authority alone can determine the reality and import of types--a view that applies with equal force against prophecy. The idea that God can not inspire people to discern truth not openly stated in the Bible

Classification of Typology

Personal types, these are person in Scripture whose lives and experiences illustrate some principle or truth of redemption. These include Adam, who is expressly described as the "figure of him that was to come" (Rom 5:14), Melchizedek, Abraham, Aaron,Joseph, Jonah, etc.

Historical /Event types, these include historical events in the Old Testament that have striking correspondence and are shadows e.g. the Exodus the Call of Abraham; Deliverances by the Judges, etc.

Ritual types,these include the sanctuary and sacrificial system in the Old Testament such as the Altar, the High Priest, the Priesthood, the Tabernacle and its furniture.
