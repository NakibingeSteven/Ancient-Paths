Spiritual Isreal

The future of literal, physical Israel

Years later, Paul wrote to believing Gentiles: “Therefore remember that you, once Gentiles in the flesh… (Ephesians 2:11-16). Here Paul is quite clear. Believing non-Jews were “once Gentiles…aliens from the commonwealth of Israel.” But “now in Christ Jesus,” Jews and Gentiles have become “one.”

“By which, when you read, you may understand my knowledge in the mystery of Christ, which in other ages was not made known to the sons of men, as it has now been revealed by the Spirit to His holy apostles and prophets: that the Gentiles should be fellow heirs, of the same body” (Ephesians 3:4-6).

“There is neither Jew nor Greek, there is neither slave nor free, there is neither male nor female; for you are all one in Christ Jesus” (Galatians 3:28).

Pastors often say during marriage ceremonies, “What God has joined together, let no man separate!” This now applies to Jews and Gentiles in Jesus Christ.

Paul poses a question about the future of the unbelieving Jews in relation to the Gospel, he writes “Has God cast away His people?” (Romans 11:1). These words are being quoted around the world to prove that God has not cast away any Israelites of the flesh. How can we explain this?

Some physical Israelites cast out

The same chapter does describe “the casting away” of some Jewish people who don't believe (Romans 11:15).

The end time application of Jezebel, the seven thousand and Elijah

Paul refers to Israel's apostasy in the days of Elijah. God told His prophet, “I have reserved for myself seven thousand men who have not bowed the knee to Baal” (Romans 11:4).

In Elijah's time there were also two Israels: One followed Baal, the other followed God.

Then Paul made this pointed application. “Even so then, at this present time there is a remnant according to the election of grace” (Romans 11:5).

Just as in Elijah's time there was a faithful remnant of Israel, even so in Paul's time there was also a faithful remnant of believing Jews, who, like himself, had been saved by grace. Based on the context of Romans 11:1-5, it is this faithful remnant of Israel, made up of Jews like Paul, which God has certainly not “cast away.”

This exact issue is revealed in the Book of Revelation. As in the days of Elijah, we are now in the midst of a terrible apostasy. There exists terrible woman Jezebel (mystery Babylon) who is leading the world astray. Yet today God has His “seven thousand” who have not “bowed the knee to Baal.”

They are His faithful remnant, His Israel in the Holy Spirit, who rely on God's power instead of their flesh. Like Elijah, they will be on the side of Jesus Christ and His truth at Armageddon.

Literal Isreal Remnant

Not all of Literal Isreal was divorced as many Jews were a part of the church and they welcomed their Messiah.

  • All 12 disciples were Jewish.
  • The Holy Spirit on the day of Pentecost fell only on Jews.
  • It was 3,000 Jews who were then baptized (see Acts 2:5, 22, 36, 41, 46).
  • The early Church in Jerusalem was Jewish.
  • Soon “a great many of the priests were obedient to the faith” (Acts 6:7).
  • Apart from Luke, the entire New Testament was written by Jews.

The question must be asked, “Should we call this group of Jewish believers in Jerusalem 'Israel' or 'the Churc''?” It's obvious they were both!

When Spirit-filled Stephen looked back into history, what did he call Israel in Moses' time? He called Israel, “the church in the wilderness” (Acts 7:38, KJV, emphasis added). Thus, according to

Stephen—whose backward glance was enlightened by Holy Ghost perception Old Testament Israel was God's Church!

The early church gentile confusion

As the early Jewish Church expanded into the Roman world, this question was finally raised: “Is our Messiah only for us? What about the Gentiles?” Then, unexpectedly, God's Spirit fell on non-Jews (see Acts 10:44-45).

Slowly, narrowness and prejudice broke down. A Jewish council of believers convened in Jerusalem to discuss “the Jew-Gentile issue” (see Acts 15)
